## $BTC and the market overall

The first part of May has been rather quiet so far for **BTC**. It reached new highs at $83k at the beginning of the month, up 38% from the February bottom. But it didn’t managed to go higher after this May 6 top, which does signal weakness, especially in a context where the US equities market (SPY and QQQ) has been absolutely up only.

The AI mania is not stopping, and the memory names have been going crazy since the Iran war local bottom: **MU (Micron)** went up +160% in 40 days, **SNDK (Sandisk)** went up +185%, the **DRAM** ETF reached record inflows (it even beat IBIT’s successful launch). MU was the most traded stock with almost $60bn volume two days in the row, so if you gather all these signals together, it makes sense to expect that some of these memory stocks have had a local top here.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!V3bF!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fed0661d8-e7d7-44a8-ac01-bb65bd725395_2184x1578.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!V3bF!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fed0661d8-e7d7-44a8-ac01-bb65bd725395_2184x1578.png)

But let’s get back to BTC: **Strategy** had its earnings calls early in the month, and for the first time ever, Saylor signaled that they were very likely to **sell some BTC** to pay their dividends, to “inoculate the idea to the market”. As usual, people are trading BTC based on the **STRC** flows, which are at a maximum right before ex-dividend date, and then usually go to zero for several day after that. As a consequence, it makes sense to get positioned short on the day before ex-div, and wait for the sell-off that could happen afterwards because of the lack of STRC inflows into BTC. That’s exactly what happened this time (but not in May), with BTC dumping from $82k to $76k in 4 days. Yesterday, Saylor announced that they’ve been buying $2bn worth of BTC last week.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xzAP!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F30524f93-6c53-48b5-abe4-7e67e7146413_2178x1576.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!xzAP!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F30524f93-6c53-48b5-abe4-7e67e7146413_2178x1576.png)

As for **$ETH**, **ETHBTC** has been down only (-15%) since mid May, and Tom Lee from Bitmine (main ETH buyer in the market) signaled that they might slow down their purchases.

## HYPE strength

**$HYPE** has been under-performing the market during the first 2 weeks of May (despite launching HIP-4 on May 2), as it’s been flat or bleeding while the altcoin market was hot, but the polarity switched completely on May 14, when Circle announced that USDC would replace USDH and that HyperLiquid would now collect all the yield of the USDC sitting on their platform. All of that will go into buying back $HYPE, and we’re talking about more than $100M+ additional buybacks. It pumped HYPE from $39 to $47 in less than a day.

At the same time, **Hyperliquid** got a lot of attention from being the most liquid place to trade **$CBRS** pre-IPO. One day later, some FUD hit: CME and NYSE are pushing the US to regulate HyperLiquid, due to concerns about market manipulation and sanctions evasion. HYPE almost retraced its entire pump in 2 days, but then pumped again yesterday, because TradeXYZ has listed the pre-IPO **SpaceX** contract. A lot of people are now bullish HYPE short-term because of the attention that the SPCX perp might get.

## ZEC is really back

**$ZEC** has had a phenomenal May performance during the first week of May, as it pumped almost 2x with barely any drawdown, from $340 to $640. It’s particularly impressive because it clearly invalidates the downtrend it’s been in since its $750 top in November. [As I tweeted](https://x.com/thedefivillain/status/2053511403273658486), what happens most of the time when a coin pumps very hard and captures a high mindshare is that it puts a top that is very hard to break again. Then you usually get a lower high (ENA, APT), or a double top (TAO, WIF). In some rare cases the second top goes higher by several digit percents (HYPE, PEPE, BONK). Here with ZEC we have a lot of people still calling for like 5x-10x. Not impossible, but seems very unlikely.

**$DASH** and **$DUSK** are two “privacy narrative” coins that benefitted from the ZEC pump too. DASH pumped +70% but has retraced most of its move, and DUSK is up 2x in two months, at a slow pace.

On May 12, it got listed on Upbit, which marked the top with a squeeze to $19.5, followed by a -33% drawdown to sub $13. VVV managed to go up 20x from its December bottom, which makes it the top performing non scam coin year to date so far, by a wide margin.

## Pavel Durov pumps TON

One of the strongest narratives in large caps lately has been the ‘revival’ of **$TON** after **Pavel Durov** tweeted that Telegram would replace the TON Foundation as the primary driving force behind the TON blockchain and become the network’s largest validator, while also confirming that fees on the TON network had dropped sixfold. The overall feeling of the market participants was ‘wow Pavel Durov wants to pump $TON, and Telegram has huge distribution, so bullish’. Which is why TON pumped relentlessly (+115%) for 3 days. It topped right below $3, and has been bleeding since then. The TON Binance perp contract reached a new record at $1.5bn (which often happens at a local top), and I suspect a lot of TON buyers have been trapped since then.

## $LAB: 10x squeeze to $8bn FDV

The main scam coin of May so far has been **$LAB**. After a ‘slow’ +230% move in April, it suddenly squeezed very hard in early April, and basically 6x’d in a day… before retracing the entire move… and then pumping 10X again from $650M to almost $8bn FDV. The overall behavior of LAB is quit strange, because so far it seems topped but it’s also not dying, and it also is the only scam coin that became this large (FDV wise) while constantly having a positive funding! ZachXBT has been going after LAB, but it absolutely didn’t prevent the price from going higher anyway, because CEXs don’t care about scam coins and are even happy about their existence.

The second most noticeable crime coin move has been $**SKYAI**, which went up 25x from its February bottom to its May highs, and up 60x from its 2025 bottom. It topped in early April without a climax around $850M FDV. Among coins that appear to be manipulated because of their low float, we can also mention **$GUA**, which is up 13x year-to-date despite no one knowing anything about this coin.

## A ton of (more or less short-lived) altcoin pumps and squeezes

We’ve been reviving a ton of altcoins in May, we can’t really say if it’s the beginning of a new uptrend or just a mechanical bounce to attack ‘comfortable shorts’ after weeks of ranging, but most of the moves overshot and then corrected.

- **Ondo** announced its selection for DTCC’s Industry Working Group on May 4, 2026. The group will support the design of tokenization services for U.S. capital markets. The news allowed **$ONDO** to finally break its very strong $0.29 three month resistance, and the coin pumped +70% in a week. It then had a -30% correction, and yesterday it pumped a bit again on the news that the SEC is expected to release its “innovation exemption” for tokenized stocks as soon as this week. $ONDO has a ton of unlocks, which definitely doesn’t help its price action.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!258A!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff6e35bde-a016-40ef-9417-8d1d2835d9bd_2178x1576.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!258A!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff6e35bde-a016-40ef-9417-8d1d2835d9bd_2178x1576.png)

- **$SUI** is one of the main large caps of the cycle, and it’s been underperforming for months, so it’s not surprising that it finally bounces again on a sign of strength in the market. $SUI pumped from $1 to $1.4 in two days but retraced the whole move.
- **$INJ** pumped +75% in May and had several catalysts: Circle launched native USDC on Injective + community buyback (almost precisely at the local top) + partnership with Musicow to tokenize music IP.
- **$ICP** had a nice and clean +75% Christmas tree pattern over a few days.
- **$LUNC** pumped +240% from its April bottom prices but topped on May 5.
- **$STRK** pumped ~50% in 3 days after the May 7 governance approval of strkBTC (a federated BTC wrapper that makes Bitcoin stakable on Starknet), which then went live on May 12. Move fully retraced…

Then there were even more short-lived pumps that were very intense and strong squeezes, but that rarely lasted more than one day:

- **$SAGA** had a +250% squeeze in a day, and it was doable to short it quite close to its top by just waiting for the volume to “saturate” on the Binance perps contract, as the highest volume 15min candle (almost $50M) marked the pico-top and it went down only from there.
- **$NIL** had a +150% move in one day, it also fully retraced in one week.
- **$BABY** squeezed 2x in 2 days, before collapsing.
- **$B3** pumped 8x bottom to top in May. The last leg happened after the Upbit listing, and it’s been consistently bleeding from there.

One of the most anticipated launches of the year happened on April 30: **$MEGA**. **MegaETH** is an Ethereum L2 that is supposed to be very ‘high performance’. Unfortunately for them, no one wants to buy L2s anymore, so despite a pump on pre-markets to $2bn FDV, the coin has absolutely been down only since launch, it’s currently trading right above $800M FDV.

## What else happened in the altcoin market ?

- On May 5, **Jito** has announced the upcoming launch of their new platform **JTX**, which is intented to become the new ‘place to trade on Solana’. The very initial reaction was a sell the news effect for a few hours, before **$JTO** started bouncing very strongly, as it then moved up 2x in 2 days. It’s also been helped by SOLBTC being surprisingly strong at the same time, with a +15% move in a week. JTO has been bleeding non-stop from its recent highs though.
- **$PENDLE** went up +65% in May to its top, mostly because its benefitting from the ‘STRC narrative’. Both Apyx and Saturn are 2 protocols tokenizing STRC shares (through proxies) and they are growing fast. They both have pools on Pendle, which makes Pendle a sort of bet on STRC growth.
- **$BCH** had its Layla hard fork on May 15. BCH has been an extremely disappointing coin since February, as it’s been incapable of finding any form of strength. This has been even more true in May, with BCH going down from $450 to as low as $350 today. The sell the news trade after the fork worked very well, even if most of the move down happened today.
- **$CHZ** is doing okay-ish in May, it’s not really pumping but doesn’t seem to follow the market when we go down. The reason for that is that the soccer World Cup is going to start in a month, and this has always been a strong catalyst for Chiliz.
- **$CFG**, a RWA protocol, seems to be one of the tokens that is among the best proxy for the CLARITY act being resolved, and it seems to be getting more and more likely that Clarity will pass. CFG went up +70% in early May, 3x from its March lows.
- **$ASTEROID**, the “SpaceX mascot” Shiba, has been consolidating between $110M and $180M mcap for one month, which tends to signal that it will probably end up breaking out higher, especially now that we know that the SpaceX IPO will happen in early June. Strong and main catalyst for ASTEROID, it seems logical for it to pump into the event and then dumping hard.
- **$STABLE** and **$H** are two very strange coins that are at the frontier between scam coins and organic coins. I think that neither of them could be sent to $20bn FDV like MYX/RAVE/COAI, but we can also clearly see in their charts that they don’t move organically, and that there are not a lot of sellers. In May, $STABLE has reached new highs (from its bottom) at $4.3bn FDV. Given the chart which shows the lack of sellers, I’d expect $STABLE to go higher. $H is a bit different because it already pumped to $4bn FDV at its ATH, and I don’t know if it could top that, but it’s just been consistently up from early April, from $800M to $3bn FDV.
- **Unibase** seems to be an AI agent protocol, its token **$UB** has pumped 12x from the April bottom to its May highs. It’s already down almost -50% from the top, and I don’t know how ‘organic’ this coin is.
