# Main events that happened in May:

- On May 27, **DTCC** announced it will connect its upcoming tokenized securities platform to the **Stellar blockchain**. This acted as a massive catalyst for **$XLM**, which had been bleeding for a while, so it had a lot of shorts to squeeze, and it pumped 2x in 3 days, which is very significant given its size.
- On May 4th, **Pavel Durov** posted: ‘Telegram replaces the TON Foundation as the driving force behind TON and becomes its largest validator’. That was a strong bullish signal for **$TON**, because it’s a coin that’s been forgotten for months if not 2 years, and it had been ranging at the lows around $1.2 for a while. It pumped +115% in 4 days, but it topped there and has been bleeding endlessly after that.

- **$MEGA** had its TGE on April 30. It opened between $1.5bn and $2bn FDV… and has been absolutely down only since launch, one of the most consistent downtrends I’ve ever seen. The other TGE that happened in May was **$BILL**, the token of Billions Network (decentralized identity project). It pumped immediately after the launch from $600M to almost $2.3bn FDV, but then came back down to retrace the whole move.
- **Ondo Finance** said it joined the DTCC Industry Working Group on May 4. It lead to a +50% squeeze on **$ONDO** after it broke out above its $0.29 resistance.
- On May 5, **Jito** has announced the upcoming launch of their new platform **JTX**, which is intented to become the new ‘place to trade on Solana’. The very initial reaction was a sell the news effect for a few hours, before **$JTO** started bouncing very strongly, as it then moved up 2x in 2 days.
- **$BCH** conducted its **Layla fork** on May 15th. It was a catalyst that a lot of people were looking for when BCH was strong a few weeks/months ago, but it has been forgotten since then, and it didn’t pump at all in anticipation of it… But it did sell the news very hard anyway, because it literally dumped -30% in 2 weeks, which is significant for a slow coin large cap like BCH.

# Main narratives of May:

## BTC: dump after the STRC inflows

**$BTC** has been somewhat strong during the first week of May, it pumped from $76k to almost $83k, which was the top for the month. **$STRC**, which grew massively in March and April, took longer before going back to par in May. At the same time, the attention around the product has gone up massively, so there were more and more people waiting for the STRC inflows to be over to derisk or flip bearish. And that’s exactly what happened, BTC topped locally on the day before ex-dividend date when Saylor probably aped a lot of USD into BTC, and it’s been down for the second half of May, bleeding from $82k to $73k. Despite this weakness from BTC, a lot of altcoins managed to be quite strong during this second half of the month.

## HYPE new ATH above $75

**$HYPE** has been one of the main narratives of May, but that only started in the second half, after an important announcement catalysed the move higher: on May 14, it was announced that USDH would be converted into USDC and that it would become the native stablecoin of Hyperliquid, and the vast majority of the yield that Circle is getting on the USDC deposited on Hyperliquid would get used to buy back HYPE.

The other catalysts that HYPE had this month were:

- the launch of the HYPE ETFs, which have been getting a lot of inflows
- the HYPE DAT PURR having significant trading volume (meaning potential buy pressure into HYPE because of the PURR ATM)
- the listings of CBRS and SPCX pre-IPO contracts. The CBRS contract got a lot of attention around the CBRS IPO, so people are expecting the same thing to happen for SpaceX, meaning increased attention on HyperLiquid from non crypto natives.

HYPE broke its $60 ATH on May 21st, and then pushed as high as $76 on May 31st. Almost a 2x pump in one month for a $40bn FDV coin… impressive!

## The AI narrative

The only narrative that seems to move several coins from the same category at the same time seems to be the AI narrative. It’s begun in 2023 and it tends to come and go in different waves, not the same coins each time:

- **$VVV** is the top performing non-scam coin year to date, after 3 weeks of consolidation below $10, it broke out very strongly in May, and quickly reached almost $20. It is now trading in a huge range between $14 and $20.
- **$NEAR** has had a phenomenal run, it pumped from $1.25 at the bottom to almost $3 at the top, with very limited drawdowns. NEAR benefits from both the AI and the privacy narrative.
- **$GRASS** had a nice 2x pump from $0.3 to $0.6 during the second half of May.
- **$AKT** pumped 2x in May after 6 weeks of rounded bottoming.
- **$NIL** had a +140% squeeze in two days.

## ZEC comeback

ZEC has done a great comeback in 2 months. After finding a base in February-March above $200, it started pumping again in April and reached almost $400 again. Then in early May, ZEC had another very strong 2 squeeze, which pushed it to $600. On May 20, it even reached $680, a level that it actually touched 4 times without being able to break it.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!M-WS!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fef138583-f382-4a8b-92f3-463c08ef6207_2240x1574.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!M-WS!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Fef138583-f382-4a8b-92f3-463c08ef6207_2240x1574.png)

ZEC has become a major coin in terms of mindshare and volume, and people were starting to call for prices of $1000 or more. Unfortunately, it collapsed -60% in early June after it was announced that there was vulnerability for years that could have theoretically allowed anyone to mint tokens infinitely without people realizing it.

## $LAB, the crime coin of May

**$LAB** is clearly the scam coin of the month. It went up 3x in April, printing higher lows, which was already a promising “suspicious” price action. It got confirmed in early May with a brutal 6x squeeze in two days… which got fully retraced… before it pumped back up again. $LAB reached $4.5bn FDV on May 6, and then it roughly ranged there for 3 weeks, with a squeeze to $8bn on May 11 that fully dumped back.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mfsV!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6798b35a-9065-458e-8f74-b765297e5ef2_2240x1570.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!mfsV!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F6798b35a-9065-458e-8f74-b765297e5ef2_2240x1570.png)

The real massive scam move actually happened in late May/early June, because it started going up very strongly with almost no drawdown, and the funding rate, which had always been neutral until then, went heavily negative. That’s what a typical scam move (like MYX, COAI, AIA, RAVE) looks like, and it did reach the same crazy valuations as these coin, since $LAB topped around $25bn FDV in early June. Absolute shame that this happens so regularly with CEXes allowing the crime.

## USELESS parabolic move

**USELESS** has had a phenomenal performance in May: it pumped from $40M to $85M market cap in 2 weeks. It then had a significant correction but quickly came back, printed higher lows, and then even managed to squeeze to $110M mcap at the beginning of June.

[![](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7Maa!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F91f4dd33-46f8-4546-92e6-e6973b016c1d_2242x1576.png)](https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!7Maa!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F91f4dd33-46f8-4546-92e6-e6973b016c1d_2242x1576.png)

USELESS has Bonk guy permanently shilling it, which makes it a great Schelling coins among memecoins, and at a low valuation. So I’m not too surprised that it finally had a strong move, after months of struggling. USELESS is the only relevant memecoin that performed well in May. (There was also a 4x move on $GIGA by the way, but it has a much smaller mindshare.)

## What else happened in May?

- **$INJ** pumped +105% in May and had several catalysts: Circle launched native USDC on Injective + community buyback (almost precisely at the local top) + partnership with Musicow to tokenize music IP. That being said I think the main catalyst was simply the fact that INJ started printing a very bullish chart, which attracted a ton of momentum traders
- **$BIO** pumped hard as a “peptides narrative trade” in April, then consolidated for 2 weeks before having a second leg in late April / early May. It topped in early May after an overall 4x move in one month. I expected it to remain stronger for longer, but that’s a tough ask for a narrative trade with no real fundamental value…
- **$PENDLE** was a very strong performer at the beginning of the month because it became a proxy for the STRC growth, as the two main STRC DeFi wrappers (Apyx and Saturn) have pools on Pendle. It pumped +75% in ten days, but then retraced the whole move at the end of the month, with STRC struggling to get back to peg.
- **$LUNC** had continuation on its April strength, it squeezed +75% during the first 5 days of May, but then topped there. Its full move from bottom to top in one month was +240%, and I honestly don’t think there was any real strong catalyst behind it.
- **$STRK** pumped ~50% in 3 days after the May 7 governance approval of strkBTC (a federated BTC wrapper that makes Bitcoin stakable on Starknet), which then went live on May 12. Move fully retraced though…
